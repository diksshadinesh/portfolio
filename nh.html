The Normal Heart: Typographic Experiment

scroll
ABOUT

The Normal Heart is TV movie based on a play by the same name by Larry Kramer, it tells

the story of Ned Weeks played by Mark Rufallo, an activist that shines light on the HIV-AIDS crisis in 1980s New York City.

The book is designed as an typographic experiment to shine light on the movie and the HIV-AIDS crisis.

The book has the script of the movie with different type treatments to convey the emotion of the words.

The book is geared towards adults living in the United States, specifically New York, LA and Atlanta ages 30-35 who work in the film industry and like political films and plays. It will be available for purchase at book stores and Amazon.

Visual concepts

Human beings use fluorescence and bright colors to get attention, much like the men in the movie, trying to get the attention of the public and government. The colored papers used in this book are inspired by our use of fluorescence and also play homage to the use of cheap paper used by activists all over the world to create posters. Lastly, the color scheme is also reminiscent of the rainbow.
